Why Ridgeline

A roaster, a cafe, and a regular table.

Ridgeline started with one drum roaster and a stubborn belief that good coffee should taste like where it came from. We buy in small lots, roast to the bean instead of to a clock, and pull every shot to order.

The cafe is the front room of the roastery, so the coffee in your cup was probably roasting while you slept. Come for the espresso, stay for the window seat.

What's in the cup

Three things we don't cut corners on.

Sourced with names

We buy from importers who can tell us the farm, the elevation, and the family. If we can't trace it, we don't roast it.

Roasted to the bean

Every origin gets its own roast curve, dialed by taste, not a timer. Small batches mean nothing sits long enough to go stale.

Poured by people who care

Our baristas train for weeks before they touch the bar. They'll remember your order — and probably your dog's name too.

Our story

It started with one roaster and a bad cup of coffee.

Eight years ago, two friends got tired of driving forty minutes for a decent espresso. So they bought a used drum roaster, taught themselves the craft, and opened the front door.

The short version

A neighborhood roastery, on purpose.

We could ship beans from a warehouse and call it a day. Instead we kept everything under one roof: the roaster hums in the back, the espresso machine runs out front, and the same hands touch both.

That choice means we can't be the cheapest or the biggest. It also means every cup is fresher than it has any right to be, and every regular gets to watch their coffee get made. We're good with that trade.

The original drum roaster
How we got here

A few years, one cup at a time.

2017

The first roast

A secondhand roaster in a rented garage and a lot of beans we got wrong before we got them right.

2019

Doors open

We moved into the old hardware store on Main and started pulling shots for actual humans. The window seats filled up fast.

2022

Direct trade

We started buying small lots straight from two cooperatives we'd visited in person. Better coffee, fairer prices, real relationships.

Today

Still small, still here

One roastery, one cafe, a tight crew, and a counter full of regulars. Exactly the size we want to be.
